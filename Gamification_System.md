## 🐉 Boss Fights (Weekly Challenges)

Every week, pick ONE problem that is **200 rating above your comfort zone**. This is your boss fight.

### Boss Fight Rules
1. Spend **at most 1 hour** trying the problem without any hints
2. If stuck after 1 hour, read **only the first hint** from the editorial
3. Try for another 30 min
4. If still stuck, read the full editorial, then implement from scratch
5. **Mark the problem for revisit** in 2 weeks

### Boss Fight XP Rewards
| Outcome | XP |
|---------|-----|
| Solved without hints | +300 XP 🏆 |
| Solved with 1 hint | +200 XP |
| Solved with full editorial + implement | +100 XP |
| Couldn't solve even after editorial | +30 XP (for trying) |

## 📖 Explain to Learn (Teach-Back System)

> **If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it well enough.**
> The fastest way to build deep understanding is to TEACH what you learn.

### After EVERY Problem: Write a 3-Line Explanation

After solving any problem, write these 3 lines in a notebook or file:

```
Problem: [Link]
Key Idea: [The ONE insight that cracked it — e.g., "parity invariant", "binary search on answer"]
Gotcha: [What tripped me up — e.g., "forgot to handle n=1 edge case"]
```

### After EVERY Topic: Write a Mini-Blog (5-10 sentences)

After finishing a lesson or topic, write a short explanation as if teaching a friend:
1. What is this technique?
2. When should I use it? (What problem characteristics signal it?)
3. What's the template / mental model?
4. What's a common mistake?

### Teach-Back XP Bonuses

| Activity | XP Bonus |
|----------|----------|
| Write 3-line explanation after a problem | +10 XP |
| Write a mini-blog for a topic | +50 XP |
| Explain a concept verbally to someone | +100 XP |
| Post a tutorial/editorial on Codeforces | +200 XP |
| Record yourself explaining a technique | +150 XP |

### The "Rubber Duck" Rule

If no friend is available: explain the solution **out loud** to an imaginary audience (or a rubber duck). Verbalizing forces your brain to organize the information, which strengthens neural pathways (myelin sheath development).

### Spaced Teaching

Every Sunday during your weekly review, pick 2 problems from 2+ weeks ago and re-explain them from memory. If you can't → re-solve them. This locks the idea into long-term memory.

## 💡 Anti-Quit Strategies

### The "2-Problem Minimum" Rule
On days you don't feel like it, make a deal: just solve 2 easy problems. Usually momentum kicks in and you'll do more. If not, you still kept your streak.

### The "20-Minute Rule"
If stuck on a problem for 20 minutes with zero progress, move to a different problem. Come back later. Fresh eyes solve problems faster.

### The "Public Commitment" Trick
Tell someone your goal. Post your streak count somewhere. Accountability = consistency.

### The "Reward Schedule"
- After every 500 XP → treat yourself to something small
- After every level up → reward yourself (food, break, game, etc.)
- After every phase complete → BIG reward (buy something, day off, etc.)

### The "Difficulty Dial" 
Feeling burned out? Dial difficulty DOWN for a week. Solve easy problems fast. Build confidence back. Then ramp up again.

### The "Study Buddy" System
Find someone at a similar level. Do virtual contests together. Race to solve problems. Competition + camaraderie = fun.

### The "Spaced Repetition" Rule
Every 2 weeks, re-solve 5 problems from 2 phases ago. This locks in long-term memory and strengthens your myelin sheath.
